Environmental Issues

There are many environmental issues that have different effects on the world we live in. Learn about them here then go to the next page to find out how we can help.

  • Fossil Fuel Dependence

    Our world relies very heavily on fossil fuels that are detrimental to drill and release CO2 when burned. This CO2 is a greenhouse gas, trapping heat in our atmosphere.

  • Plastic Pollution

    In 2015, we produced 419 million tons of plastic. About 14 million tons per year ends up in the ocean per year. Plastic breaks down to microplastics and these deplete the overall water quality. Plastic products also do not biodegrade, so they will be there forever unless we do something about it.

  • Climate Change

    Climate change has brought upon many negative impacts to our environment such as droughts and flooding. Enviroments such as the arctic are most vulnerable due to warming being twice as fast as the global average. Many environments home to a variety of species aresuffering from climate change. Invasive species may start to come thrive in affected environments due to the environment being more detail because of climate change.

  • Ocean Acidification

    Ocean Acidification refers to oceans absorbing 30% of carbon dioxide released into the Earth's atmosphere. PH levels lower and if they become too low, organisms that thrive in the ocean such as oysters could have their shells and skeletons dissolve. Rising global temperatures and plastic pollution also cause ocean acidification. If other environmental issues are not limited, then more issues like ocean acidification will appear more and more.

  • Deforestation

    Through limiting deforestation can forests continue to thrive and avoid extinction. With the help of people willingly planting seeds for trees near forests going through deforestation to grow, certain parts of forests will be limited from being cut down while the other parts are permitted so the effects of forests being able to combat soil erosion will still be present while people can still cut down trees without impacting the environment more negatively.
